## **Information on BA Theses** 

The following information applies to candidates who would like to write their BA Thesis with Prof. Dr. Daniela Landert. 

## **First steps** 

Carefully read the regulations on writing your BA Thesis in the _Studienführer:_ - http://www.as.uni heidelberg.de/studienfuehrer.php 

If you are unsure about what topic to work on, I recommend that you read up on different areas of research. A good starting point is Wray and Bloomer (2006): 

Wray, Alison & Aileen Bloomer. 2006. _Projects in Linguistics. A Practical Guide to Researching Language_ . 2nd Edition. London: Hodder Education. 

To find out more about different areas, read relevant introductory textbooks and look at handbooks that provide an in-depth discussion of different fields of research. 

Once you have a first idea of a topic or topic area in which you would like to write your thesis, as well as the approximate time frame, come to my office hours to discuss your ideas. 

## **Sign-up procedure** 

_NOTE: In order to sign up for your BA thesis, you need to have completed at least 57 CP (for 50/50 BA) or 83 CP (for 75/25 BA)._ 

You can find all the administrative information about signing up for the BA Thesis here: https://www.neuphil.uni-heidelberg.de/de/studium/gemeinsames-pruefungsamt-derphilosophischen-und-neuphilologischen-fakultaet/bachelorpruefungen 

You can start at any date, provided you fulfil the necessary requirements. There are no signup deadlines. However, note that if the BA Thesis is your final assessment, you have to sign up for your BA Thesis at the latest 8 weeks after completing the final exam. 

_NOTE: Once the form is submitted, you need to complete and submit your BA Thesis within 9 weeks! You can apply once for a two-week extension._ 

## **Requirements concerning form and content** 

Your BA Thesis can be written in any area of English Linguistics. It has to present an empirical study, in which you analyse data and present results. 

The length of your BA Thesis should be 8,000 to 12,000 words (typically 20–30 pages). 

Please consult the Section on “How to Write a Term Paper” in the Studienführer. 

_NOTE: Please pay special attention to avoid any forms of plagiarism. Wray and Bloomer (2006), mentioned above, has an excellent chapter that discusses common problems that lead to plagiarism, and how to avoid them!_

## **Learning objectives and grading** 

The following table gives an overview of the skills you are expected to demonstrate in your BA Thesis. They form the basis of evaluation: Each skill will be assessed separately and the final grade is calculated according to the weighting that’s indicated. 

|**Criterion**|**Learning objective**|
|---|---|
|**Research question**<br>**(20%)**|Develop a research question that is appropriate for the context of<br>the thesis (i.e. fits the topic, is relevant for linguistic research, can<br>be answered on the basis of the available data, can be dealt with<br>in 8,000–12,000 words)|
|**Argumentation**<br>**(20%)**|Developing an argument in a coherent, well-structured and<br>convincing way;<br>Supporting your argument by presenting evidence in the form of<br>relevant data|
|**Data analysis**<br>**(20%)**|Using appropriate methods for analysing your data;<br>Presenting your data in a formally correct and meaningful way|
|**Secondary literature**<br>**(20%)**|Searching for and Selecting relevant secondary literature for your<br>research topic;<br>Interpreting secondary literature in a critical way|
|**Formal aspects**<br>**(10%)**|Quoting sources in a formally correct way (No plagiarism! Correct<br>formatting);<br>Formatting of paper follows guidelines for English linguistics, as<br>specified in the Studienführer|
|**Language**<br>**(10%)**|Writing a paper in reasonably good English|
|**GRADE**|**(rounded to one-third grade)**|
